Education

Gallagher attended Parkland High School from September 2018 to June 2022 and graduated with a 3.9 GPA. He earned High Honor Roll every semester. He also participated in football and pole vaulting and was an active member of both the school's Computer Club and Dungeons & Dragons Club. Starting in 2022, he attended the Honors College at Bloomsburg University (now Commonwealth University) for two semesters before transferring to Lehigh Carbon Community College for his upcoming Fall 2023 semester.

Work experience

From January to March in 2020, Gallagher was a cashier and usher at AMC Tilghman Square 8 in Allentown. He would sell and stock products at the concession stand. He would later sell tickets, configure pricing, and handle refunds for customers from the front ticket stand. As an usher, Gallagher would sweep, sanitize, and repair seats in the theater rooms in-between movies. He was trained to refill and repair drink machines and would have to control theaters' lighting before and after movies. This position was terminated due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

From August 2020 to February 2022, Gallagher worked as a cashier and shift leader at YoFresh Yogurt Café.[1] He would open, close, and oversee the store as a stand-in manager. He was also trained to disassemble, clean, and reassemble the store's machinery for producing frozen yogurt and rolled ice cream. Most commonly he would scoop and refill toppings for a variety of customers, including young children, creating a positive and welcoming environment while doing so. He also occasionally coordinated coupon/discount deals with other businesses in the shopping center.

While attending his second semester at Bloomsburg University (February to June 2023), Gallagher worked as a host at Marley's Brewery & Grille.[2] He would greet customers and bring them to their tables. The job also included bussing tables, restocking silverware, and petting customers' dogs on the patio. During this time, Gallagher was also involved with a digital humanities project with his Honors professor for a period of about three months.[3] The position involved scanning local historical documents such as scrapbooks, magazines, and architectural blueprints to upload to an online database. He would often write Excel macros and image processing scripts.
